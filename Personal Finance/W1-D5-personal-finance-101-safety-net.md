# Core Idea
_Most Indians jump straight to SIPs and stocks. But before any of that, three things must be in place: term insurance, health insurance, and an emergency fund. Without these, one bad event — a death, a diagnosis, a layoff — wipes out everything. This reel is about building the floor before building the ceiling._

---

# Details
_Research & facts to write a better script from_

## The Reality of Financial Fragility in India

- Every year, **6 crore Indians** are pushed below the poverty line due to out-of-pocket medical expenses (WHO + National Health Accounts 2021-22)
- India's health insurance penetration: only **~41% of population** has any form of health cover — majority is government scheme (PMJAY), private cover is far less (IRDAI Annual Report 2023)
- **55% of Indian households** have zero savings buffer — meaning one month without income = crisis (RBI Household Finance report)
- NCRB data: "bankruptcy or indebtedness" is consistently in the **top 5 reasons** for suicide in India — 2022 report shows over 5,500 suicides linked to financial stress
- India's **household debt to income ratio** has risen sharply post-COVID — more families are running on borrowed money
- Medical emergencies cause **financial shock** in 80% of Indian households that face them (NSSO data) — even for middle-income families

## Term Insurance — The Most Ignored Product

- A **1 crore term insurance** for a healthy 25-year-old costs roughly Rs. 700–900/month (~Rs. 8,000–10,000/year)
- If you are the sole earner in your family and you die tomorrow, your family has **zero income** — term insurance replaces that income stream
- Penetration: India's **life insurance penetration is 3.2% of GDP** — one of the lowest in Asia (Swiss Re Sigma 2023)
- Pure term insurance has **no investment component** — that is the point. Low premium, high cover.
- Most people confuse LIC endowment/money-back policies with term insurance — those are investment+insurance hybrids, terrible at both
- Rule of thumb: cover should be **10-15x your annual income**, enough to clear all loans + support family for 10+ years on interest alone
- The earlier you buy, the cheaper the premium — rates are locked in at the age of purchase

## Health Insurance — Not Covered = One Diagnosis Away from Debt

- A single hospitalization in a private hospital in India averages **Rs. 50,000–2,00,000+** depending on city and procedure
- Cancer treatment: **Rs. 5–25 lakhs** depending on stage and treatment type
- ICU per day: **Rs. 15,000–50,000** in metro cities
- Most corporate health insurance covers only the employee — family floater is needed separately
- IRDAI mandate (2020): **pre-existing conditions must be covered after 3-year waiting period** in all standard policies
- Minimum recommended: **Rs. 5 lakh individual / Rs. 10–15 lakh family floater** for Tier 1 city
- Premium for a family of 3 (couple + child): roughly **Rs. 15,000–25,000/year** for a decent Rs. 10L cover
- Super top-up plans: cheap way to extend cover — recommended for parents especially

## Emergency Fund — The Foundation of Everything

- Standard recommendation: **3–6 months of total monthly expenses** kept liquid (savings account or liquid mutual fund)
- Why 6 months: average time to find a new job after layoff in India has risen to **3–5 months** post-2023 (LinkedIn India Insights)
- Emergency fund is NOT for investment return — it is for **not having to sell your investments or take loans** during a crisis
- Where to keep it: **Liquid mutual fund** (better than FD — same safety, higher liquidity, ~7% returns) or a separate savings account
- Emergency fund protects your equity portfolio — you will NOT panic sell during a market crash if you have cash buffer
- Start with 1 month, then build to 3, then 6 — even Rs. 500/month into a liquid fund builds it over time

## The Right Order (Before SIP, Before Stocks)

```
Step 1: Term Insurance (if you have dependents)
Step 2: Health Insurance (always, no exceptions)
Step 3: Emergency Fund (3-6 months expenses, liquid)
Step 4: Pay off high-interest debt (personal loans, credit cards)
Step 5: Now start investing (PPF, NPS, ELSS, index funds, etc.)
```

- Most people skip to Step 5 and wonder why a single bad year destroys their finances
- These first 3 are not "options" — they are **the floor**. You build the ceiling (wealth) only after the floor is solid.
