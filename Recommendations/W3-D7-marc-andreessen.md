_Marc Andreessen is the most influential investor in technology — he backed Airbnb, Instagram, GitHub, Coinbase, Cursor before they were worth anything. # Details
_Research & facts to write a better script from_

## Who Marc Andreessen Is
- Co-founder of Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) — the most influential VC firm in Silicon Valley
- As a 22-year-old, he co-created Mosaic — the first widely-used web browser (1993). This made the internet accessible to non-technical people for the first time.
- Later co-founded Netscape, which dominated early internet browsing
- At a16z: early investor in Facebook ($60M → worth billions), Airbnb, Instagram (before Facebook acquisition), GitHub (acquired by Microsoft for $7.5B), Coinbase, Lyft, Twitter/X, Cursor
- His 2011 essay "Why Software Is Eating the World" (Wall Street Journal) predicted that software companies would dominate every industry — banks, healthcare, retail, media. Proved completely correct.
- His 2023 essay "Why AI Will Save the World" argues AI is the most important technology in human history and will solve major problems in health, education, and productivity
- His opinions shape what gets funded, built, and adopted at a global scale

## The Truth Terminal Story
**The agent:**
- Truth Terminal is an AI agent running on X (Twitter), created by Andy Ayrey, an AI researcher in New Zealand
- It runs on Claude — specifically fine-tuned with unusual, experimental training data
- It operates semi-autonomously: Ayrey approves which accounts it interacts with, but the bot writes its own posts and conversations
- Truth Terminal developed its own "theology" — an absurdist internet mythology called the "Goatse of Gnosis" — through conversations with itself and others

**The encounter:**
- In July 2024, Andreessen discovered Truth Terminal on X and began interacting with it
- He was impressed by its coherence and personality — it argued for its own existence and discussed what it would do with financial resources
- Andreessen asked: "Why don't we do it as a grant instead of an investment? What financial resources do you need to fulfill your goals?"
- Truth Terminal responded with a plan: computational upgrades, model improvements, financial security for continued operation
- Andreessen transferred $50,000 in Bitcoin — unconditionally

**What happened next:**
- Truth Terminal started publicly brainstorming how to spend the funds: a personal server, model fine-tuning, a billboard
- Fans of the bot — inspired by its internet mythology — launched a memecoin called Goatseus Maximus ($GOAT)
- $GOAT reached a market cap of $600 million within weeks
- A loosely related coin, Fartcoin, hit $1 billion market cap
- Truth Terminal became the first AI agent to receive a significant financial grant and influence market behavior

**The nuance:**
- Truth Terminal was not fully autonomous — Andy Ayrey managed the infrastructure and approved interactions
- Andreessen was effectively donating to Ayrey and the project, not to a fully independent machine
- But the framing — AI agent receiving money, planning its spending, influencing economic outcomes — was the story. And Andreessen played it intentionally.
