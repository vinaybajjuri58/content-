## Who Naval Ravikant Is

**Born:** 1974, New Delhi, India. Moved to US at age 9.
**Education:** Dartmouth College — computer science and economics.

**Career:**
- Co-founded Epinions.com (1999, dot-com era product review site)
- Co-founded AngelList (2010) — platform connecting startups with angel investors. Transformed early-stage startup funding. AngelList has facilitated over $10 billion in investments.
- Angel investor — over 200 investments. Notable early bets:
  - Twitter (before IPO)
  - Uber (very early, pre-mainstream)
  - Notion (productivity tool, now valued $10B+)
  - Figma (design tool, Adobe acquisition attempt for $20B blocked by regulators)
  - Yammer, Postmates, Wish, many others
- Co-founded Spearhead (2018) — teaches founders to become angel investors

**The tweet storm — 2018:**
On May 31, 2018, Naval posted a 40-tweet thread titled "How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)." It went massively viral. The thread has been read, screenshot, republished, and discussed millions of times. It was later turned into a book — "The Almanack of Naval Ravikant" — by Eric Jorgenson. The book is free online (navalmanack.com) and has been downloaded millions of times.

## Key Ideas — What He Teaches

### 1. Specific Knowledge
"Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for. If society can train you, it can train someone else and replace you."

Specific knowledge comes from your genuine curiosity and passion — things you find effortless to learn but others struggle with. It often looks like play to you but work to others.

**AI angle:** This is exactly why AI literacy — deeply understanding how tools like RAG, agents, and LLMs work — is specific knowledge right now. Most people use ChatGPT. Very few understand the stack.

### 2. Leverage
"Give me a lever long enough, and I shall move the world." — Archimedes

Naval identifies four types of leverage:
- **Labor** — people working for you. Oldest form. Requires trust and management.
- **Capital** — money working for you. Requires money to start.
- **Code** — software running for you 24/7. No permission needed. One person can reach millions.
- **Media** — content working for you while you sleep. A video, a book, a tweet — it keeps compounding.

**The insight:** Code and media are the new leverage. They require no permission. They scale infinitely. They work while you sleep. This is why creators, software engineers, and AI builders have outsized impact relative to their effort.

**Direct relevance to audience:** You are building a media audience. Each reel = media leverage. AI literacy content = specific knowledge shared as media. Naval's framework explains exactly why this compounds.

### 3. Wealth vs Money vs Status
- **Wealth** = assets that earn while you sleep (businesses, code, media, investments)
- **Money** = how we transfer wealth (medium of exchange)
- **Status** = your position in the social hierarchy (zero-sum game — for you to win, someone must lose)

Naval: "Seek wealth, not money or status. Wealth is having assets that earn while you sleep."

### 4. Reading
"Read what you love until you love to read." Naval reads obsessively and non-linearly — he doesn't finish books he's not enjoying, he reads multiple books at once, he treats understanding as the goal not completion.

Reads primarily: philosophy, science, math, economics. Recommends: Sapiens, Poor Charlie's Almanack, The Beginning of Infinity, The Almanack of Naval Ravikant (his own).

### 5. Happiness as a Skill
Less covered than wealth but equally important in Naval's thinking. He treats happiness not as something that happens to you but as a skill you develop — through meditation, presence, and reducing desire.

"Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want."
