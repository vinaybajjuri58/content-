# Week 5 Overview

**Theme:** Knowledge Graphs — a more powerful memory structure than RAG. RAG retrieves chunks. Knowledge graphs understand *connections* between things. And the dark side: what happens when you feed AI the wrong memory.
**Bridge:** Week 4 covered how AI retrieves relevant information (RAG). Week 5 goes deeper — some data isn't just text to retrieve, it's a web of relationships. Knowledge graphs let AI reason across those connections, not just find matching passages.

## Week Narrative

- **D1:** Knowledge graph intro — childhood riddle as the analogy. Nodes and edges. How it's different from RAG (retrieves text vs understands relationships).
- **D2–D4:** Three real use cases. Legal docs: each clause is a node, cross-references are edges. E-commerce: products and customers as nodes, "bought together" as edges. Concall/movie: characters and topics as nodes, relationships and interactions as edges.
- **D5:** Dark side — wrong memory fed to AI = wrong outputs at scale. Memory poisoning, data injection, compounding errors in knowledge graphs. Connects back to Week 3 D6 (tools gone wrong) but at the memory layer.
- **D7:** The Alignment Problem — book on why AI systems do what we specify, not what we intend. Thematic close to a week about what happens when memory and reasoning go wrong.
