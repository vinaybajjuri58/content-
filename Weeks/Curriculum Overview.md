# Curriculum Overview

**Target audience:** General public — not just engineers. Every person will be affected by AI like they were by the internet. Teachers, shop owners, investors, students — all of them.

**Format:** 7 videos/week. Drop carousels as supplementary when a concept needs more depth.

**Philosophy:** Build a library, not just viral clips. Six months from now, someone wanting to understand AI in Telugu from scratch should be able to start at Week 1 and go through the whole series. Curriculum approach, not random content.

**Key insight on hooks:** General public needs a cultural/emotional entry point before the concept. Chitti Robo for prompting is the template — familiar reference first, real concept second. Apply this to every technical week.

## Week-by-Week Planning Notes

### Week 3 — Tools
- Highest follower spike expected. People already use ChatGPT and have questions.
- Cover: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Cursor
- Vibe coding gets covered here or Week 10-11 (image/video tools week) — decision pending

### Week 4 — Memory Part 1: Traditional + Context Window
- Start with the fundamental problem: AI forgets everything after the conversation ends
- Traditional memory: RAM analogy — fast but temporary
- Context window: the "attention span" of a model — how many tokens it can hold at once
- Why this matters: why your long ChatGPT conversations start going wrong near the end

### Week 5 — Memory Part 2: Vector DBs
- **Hardest week to make watchable for general public.** Terms like "embeddings," "vector space," "similarity search" are abstract.
- Need a very strong analogy — working idea: a library sorted by *meaning* not by title or author. You ask for "something about betrayal" and it finds the right book without you knowing the name.
- Use cases to cover:
  - Chat with PDF (upload your textbook, ask questions)
  - Chat with website (customer support bots)
  - Semantic search (find meaning, not just keywords)
- Lead with use cases first, explain the tech second

### Week 6 — Memory Part 3: Knowledge Graphs
- **Most creative week in the whole curriculum.**
- Use cases planned (in order of general-public friendliness):
  1. **Character graph in a movie** — start here. Take RRR or Baahubali, map characters, relationships, loyalties. Everyone gets it visually. This is the hook.
  2. **Company management graph for investing** — board members, subsidiaries, ownership chains, related parties. Telugu investor audience will find this immediately useful.
  3. **Code review graph** — relationships between modules, dependencies, what breaks what. Engineers will love this.
- Key move: show that the same graph logic applies to movies, companies, and codebases. Same concept, three different worlds.

### Week 7 — Workflows
- n8n, Zapier + AI, Make.com
- Practical automation examples relevant to Telugu audience — small business use cases

### Week 8 — Agents
- Autonomous AI — what it means for AI to "act" not just "answer"
- Connects back to Week 2 prompting and Week 4-6 memory

### Week 9 — Connecting the Dots
- This is the most important structural week
- Show how: Stack (W1) → Prompting (W2) → Tools (W3) → Memory (W4-6) → Workflows (W7) → Agents (W8) all fit together
- Reward for followers who've been there since Week 1

### Weeks 10-11 — Image + Video
- Midjourney, Sora, RunwayML, CapCut AI etc.
- Vibe coding fits here if not covered in Week 3

### Week 12 — Audio
- ElevenLabs, voice cloning, AI music (Suno, Udio)
- Telugu voice cloning angle could be very relevant and interesting for this audience

### Week 13+ — AI-First Software
- What to actually build
- By this point audience trusts you, understands foundations, ready to act
- Natural monetization point — course, community, consulting offer
